--- layout: archive title: "Research" permalink: /research/ author_profile: true --- # Research ## Research Overview My research focuses on developing mathematical and computational frameworks for understanding complex traffic systems under heterogeneous and disordered traffic conditions. The primary objective is to bridge the gap between empirical traffic observations and continuum-based traffic flow models through the integration of theory, data, and machine learning. The research combines traffic flow theory, partial differential equations, numerical methods, data-driven modeling, and artificial intelligence to investigate the dynamics of mixed traffic streams commonly observed in developing countries. --- ## Research Areas ### Two-Dimensional Traffic Flow Modeling Conventional traffic flow models are predominantly one-dimensional and lane-based. However, traffic systems in many urban environments exhibit significant lateral interactions that cannot be adequately represented using traditional formulations. Research efforts focus on: * Development of second-order two-dimensional macroscopic traffic flow models. * Modeling longitudinal and lateral vehicle interactions. * Analysis of stability and wave propagation characteristics. * Numerical solution of nonlinear conservation laws. --- ### Lane-Free and Disordered Traffic Dynamics Traffic streams in developing countries often operate without strict lane discipline. This research investigates the collective behavior emerging from such traffic conditions. Key topics include: * Lateral vehicle movement behavior. * Multi-class traffic interactions. * Congestion formation mechanisms. * Emergent traffic patterns under heterogeneous traffic compositions. --- ### Drone-Based Traffic Data Collection A large-scale aerial traffic dataset has been developed using drone-based observations of urban traffic environments. Research activities include: * Vehicle trajectory extraction. * Traffic state estimation. * Microscopic and macroscopic data analysis. * Validation of traffic flow models using empirical observations. The dataset currently contains approximately 59 hours of traffic recordings collected from multiple urban locations in India. --- ### Physics-Informed Machine Learning Recent work explores the integration of traffic flow theory with machine learning through Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). Research directions include: * Physics-constrained learning of traffic states. * Data assimilation techniques. * Hybrid model-driven and data-driven approaches. * Learning hidden traffic dynamics from sparse observations. --- ## Current Projects ### Higher-Order Continuum Modeling of Lane-Free Traffic Development of a second-order two-dimensional traffic flow model capable of representing both longitudinal and lateral traffic dynamics under heterogeneous traffic conditions. ### Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Traffic State Estimation Construction of PINN-based frameworks for estimating traffic density, velocity, and flow while satisfying conservation and momentum equations. ### Chennai Traffic Dataset Development and dissemination of large-scale drone-based traffic datasets for advancing research in traffic flow modeling, transportation analytics, and intelligent transportation systems. --- ## Research Methods * Traffic Flow Theory * Partial Differential Equations * Numerical Methods * Finite Difference Schemes * Machine Learning * Physics-Informed Neural Networks * Computer Vision for Trajectory Extraction * Statistical Data Analysis --- ## Future Directions Future research aims to integrate traffic flow theory, artificial intelligence, and real-world trajectory datasets to develop scalable digital twins of urban transportation systems capable of supporting traffic management, congestion mitigation, and mobility planning.
